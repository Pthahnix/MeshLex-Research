# MAR-3D: Progressive Masked Auto-Regressor for High-Resolution 3D Generation

## Core Architecture

**Type: Hybrid (Masked Auto-Regressive + Diffusion)**

MAR-3D combines masked autoregressive modeling with per-token diffusion denoising. It is NOT a standard next-token AR model. Instead, it uses a **Cascaded MAR** design with two stages:

- **MAR-LR**: Generates low-resolution latent tokens (256 tokens) capturing global shape structure.
- **MAR-HR**: Refines low-resolution tokens into high-resolution tokens (1024 tokens) with fine geometric details.

Both stages share the same architecture but differ in inputs. MAR-HR additionally receives the low-resolution tokens generated by MAR-LR as conditioning input.

The key architectural distinction from pure diffusion (e.g., DiT) is the decomposition of the joint distribution into temporal (diffusion) and spatial (auto-regressive) components. This decomposition enables progressive token scaling that pure diffusion approaches struggle with.

## How 3D Data is Tokenized

**Tokenization: Pyramid VAE (continuous latent space, no VQ)**

MAR-3D explicitly avoids vector quantization to prevent compression loss. Instead, it uses a **Pyramid VAE** that operates on point clouds:

1. **Encoder**: Takes multi-resolution point clouds (16384, 4096, 1024 points at 3 levels) with surface normals. Learnable queries attend to each resolution level via separate cross-attention layers, then results are summed and processed through self-attention to produce latent tokens X.
2. **Decoder**: Self-attention layers process encoded features, then cross-attention between query points (with positional embeddings) and latent tokens predicts occupancy values.
3. **Training**: BCE loss for occupancy prediction + KL-divergence for latent regularization.

The latent tokens are continuous (not discrete), and the diffusion process within MAR handles the per-token probability distribution in this continuous space.

## Multi-scale / Hierarchical Design

Two levels of hierarchy:

1. **VAE level**: Pyramid design with K=3 resolution levels of point clouds. Each level uses distinct cross-attention layers -- coarse levels capture structure, fine levels capture geometry. The pyramid VAE with 1024 tokens outperforms a single-level VAE with 2048 tokens.

2. **Generation level**: Cascaded MAR-LR (256 tokens) to MAR-HR (1024 tokens). This is a coarse-to-fine generation strategy where:
   - MAR-LR generates global shape from image conditioning
   - MAR-HR super-resolves to detailed geometry conditioned on LR tokens + image

Within each MAR stage, tokens are generated in random order using a cosine schedule (fewer tokens in early steps, more in later steps), with parallel decoding at each step.

## Conditioning Mechanism

- **Image conditioning**: Concatenated features from CLIP and DINOv2 encoders serve as initial tokens for the MAR encoder.
- **Classifier-free guidance**: 10% random dropout of conditional features during training. Linear CFG schedule during inference (lower guidance early, increasing to lambda_cfg).
- **Condition augmentation** (for MAR-HR): Gaussian noise added to low-resolution tokens to bridge the train-inference gap (VAE-encoded LR tokens during training vs. MAR-LR-generated tokens during inference). Uses t ~ U(0.4, 0.6) during training, fixed t=0.5 at inference.

## Scale

- **Training data**: 260K meshes from Objaverse (first stage), fine-tuned on 60K high-quality meshes.
- **Batch sizes**: 32/GPU for MAR-LR, 8/GPU for MAR-HR.
- **Model parameters**: Not explicitly stated in the paper. The architecture uses MAE encoder-decoder with self-attention layers plus a lightweight MLP-based diffusion denoiser.
- **Rendering**: 56 conditional views per mesh (8 base views x 7 rotations).
- **Token counts**: 256 (LR) and 1024 (HR) latent tokens.

## Key Innovation

MAR-3D introduces a cascaded masked auto-regressive framework with condition augmentation that enables progressive upscaling of 3D latent token resolution without the quadratic cost explosion of scaling tokens directly. By combining random-order masked prediction (which naturally handles unordered 3D tokens) with per-token diffusion loss (which avoids VQ compression artifacts), the method achieves both the scalability benefits of AR models and the continuous-space advantages of diffusion.

## Main Limitation

- The paper does not report model parameter counts, making it difficult to assess computational cost relative to competitors.
- The cascaded two-stage approach introduces potential error propagation from MAR-LR to MAR-HR, which condition augmentation only partially mitigates.
- The method is limited to single-image conditioning and occupancy-based mesh extraction (Marching Cubes), with no demonstrated text-to-3D capability.
- Training requires careful data curation and rotation augmentation to avoid view ambiguity, suggesting sensitivity to data preparation.
