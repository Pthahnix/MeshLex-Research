# MeshMamba: State Space Models for Articulated 3D Mesh Generation and Reconstruction

## Core Architecture

**Type**: Mamba State Space Model (SSM)

MeshMamba replaces the standard transformer backbone with Mamba-SSM blocks, achieving near-linear complexity with respect to input sequence length (versus quadratic for transformers). The architecture follows a U-ViT-inspired design with L+1 Mamba blocks organized into shallow (L/2), mid (1), and deep (L/2) groups with skip connections between the first and second groups. Each Mamba block contains a selective SSM layer, linear layers, a 1D convolution layer, and nonlinear activations. Time embeddings are injected via summation at every block.

Two downstream models are built on MeshMamba:
- **MambaDiff3D**: A denoising diffusion model for unconditional/class-conditional 3D articulated mesh generation. Uses DDIM sampling with v-prediction parameterization and cosine variance schedule.
- **Mamba-HMR**: A regression model for single-image 3D human mesh recovery, following the Mesh Transformer architecture but with Mamba blocks replacing transformer self-attention. Uses HRNet-w48 as CNN backbone.

## 3D Data Tokenization/Representation

Meshes are represented as raw vertex coordinates x in R^{N x 3}, where N can exceed 10,000 vertices. All training meshes share the same topology (connectivity) as a template mesh, so point-to-point correspondences are known.

The critical design choice is **vertex serialization** -- converting unordered 3D mesh vertices into a 1D sequence suitable for Mamba's sequential processing:

1. **Template-coordinate sorting**: Vertices are sorted by the x/y/z coordinates of their corresponding positions on a T-pose template mesh. Six serialization variants are generated by permuting axis order and sign: "xyz", "-xyz", "yzx", "-yzx", "zxy", "-zxy".
2. **DensePose IUV sorting**: Vertices are first grouped by body part segmentation (I map), then sorted within each part by U and V coordinates. The 24 body parts are sorted by centroid coordinates.

Two serialization strategies are combined per model (one applied to all layers except one, the other to the remaining layer) to balance quality and efficiency.

Additionally, the paper generates both vertex positions and surface normals, then combines them via a **Poisson system** in the gradient domain to produce smooth, detail-preserving meshes.

## Conditioning Mechanism

- **MambaDiff3D**: Timestep conditioning via summation into every Mamba block. Class-conditional generation (e.g., clothing type) is supported. Unconditional generation starts from Gaussian noise.
- **Mamba-HMR**: Image conditioning via CNN features (HRNet-w48) fed as joint queries and vertex queries with position embeddings into the Mamba blocks.

## Scale

- **Parameters**: Not explicitly stated, but comparable to transformer baselines. The Mamba architecture reduces parameters significantly by eliminating the need for mesh upsamplers -- it directly outputs full-resolution meshes (6,890 SMPL vertices or 10,475 SMPL-X vertices).
- **Training data**: SURREAL, DFAUST, CAPE, GRAB, AMASS, BARC, Animal3D for generation; Human3.6M, COCO, AGORA, BEDLAM, UBody for HMR.
- **Training compute**: 8x A100 GPUs or 6x4 V100 GPUs. Training time ~18 min/epoch for 6,890 vertices on 6x4 V100s (vs. 100 min/epoch for transformer).

## Key Innovation

MeshMamba demonstrates that Mamba-SSMs can replace transformers for dense 3D mesh processing by designing structure-aware vertex serialization strategies (body-part UV maps and template coordinates), enabling 6-9x faster inference than transformers while generating meshes with 10,000+ vertices -- a scale previously impractical for vertex-based approaches due to quadratic transformer complexity.

## Main Limitation

Limited to **fixed-topology meshes with tight clothing**. The method requires all training meshes to share the same connectivity as a template mesh (known point-to-point correspondences), which prevents handling loose/flowing clothing, varying topology, or general object categories. Generalization to unseen datasets is also limited compared to approaches trained on more diverse data.

## Quantitative Highlights

- **Generation**: 1-NNA of 53.1% on SURREAL (SOTA), outperforming DiffSurf (54.4%), VPoser (60.7%), and NRDF (81.6%).
- **Whole-body HMR**: PA-MVE of 25.9mm on UBody (fine-tuned), beating SMPLer-X-L (31.9mm) and AiOS (32.5mm).
- **Speed**: 4.5 sec for 10,475-vertex mesh (250 DDIM steps, A100) vs. 28.1 sec for transformer. ~1 sec with 50 steps.
- **Ablation**: Mamba blocks (53.1% 1-NNA) match transformers (53.6%) while being much faster; MLP (73.7%) and GNN (74.2%) fail.
